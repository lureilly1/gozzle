---
title: Privacy
description: What gozzle reads, what stays on your machine, and what it never does
---

gozzle is a **local** tool. It runs on your machine as a CLI and an MCP stdio
server. There are no gozzle-operated servers, no accounts, and **no telemetry** —
gozzle never phones home, and we cannot see your schemas, queries, or data.

This page describes exactly what gozzle touches. It is informational, not a legal
contract.

## What gozzle connects to

gozzle connects to **one thing**: the ClickHouse instance you configure through
`GOZZLE_CLICKHOUSE_URL` and its credentials. Every statement runs with
[`readonly = 2`](/docs/read-only), so gozzle can read but can never write, alter,
or drop anything — enforced at the connection layer regardless of the account's
privileges.

## What gozzle reads

gozzle reads only what a check needs:

- **Schema and layout metadata** from `system.tables`, `system.columns`, and
  `system.parts` — engines, keys, partitions, row/byte counts.
- **`SHOW CREATE TABLE`** output.
- **Bounded check results** — e.g. duplicate counts and a small sample of
  affected sorting keys for [`verify_dedup`](/docs/verify-dedup), or
  `EXPLAIN` output for [`diagnose_query`](/docs/diagnose-query).

Checks return **verdicts and proof, not bulk data**. Result rows are
[capped](/docs/guardrails) (`max_result_rows`, 10,000 by default), so gozzle
never pulls your tables down wholesale.

## What leaves your machine

<Callout type="warn" title="Your AI client sees gozzle's output">
gozzle returns its results to the AI client you connected it to (Claude Code,
Cursor, Codex, …). That client may transmit those results to its model provider,
exactly as it does for any other tool output. gozzle controls what it returns —
verdicts, counts, fingerprints, and small samples — but not what your AI client
does with them. Treat sample keys and table names as data your AI provider will
see.
</Callout>

gozzle itself sends nothing anywhere except the read-only queries it runs against
your configured ClickHouse.

## What gozzle stores locally

- **Audit log** — [opt-in](/docs/audit-log), off by default. When
  `GOZZLE_AUDIT_LOG` is set, gozzle appends one JSON line per tool call to that
  local file. Migration statements are recorded as a SHA-256
  fingerprint, never as raw SQL.
- **Local slices** — only when you explicitly run
  [`create_local_slice`](/docs/local-slices). This is the one case where real
  table data leaves ClickHouse: one complete partition is copied to disk under
  `~/.gozzle/slices` and **persists until you clean it**.

<Callout type="warn" title="Local slices contain production data">
A slice workspace holds copied source rows and table DDL, which may include
sensitive values. Protect the directory, set a retention period, and remove
slices you no longer need with `gozzle slices clean`.
</Callout>

## What gozzle never does

The points above describe the **gozzle tool** (the CLI and MCP server) running on
your machine:

- No telemetry, analytics, crash reporting, or usage tracking.
- No network calls except to your configured ClickHouse.
- No writes to your ClickHouse — ever.
- No copying of table data anywhere unless you run `create_local_slice`.

## This website

This documentation site collects nothing — there is no signup form, no account,
and no analytics or tracking. gozzle is open source, so you can verify all of the
above in the source.
